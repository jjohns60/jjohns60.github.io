Observing and Quantifying Snow Variability at Small Scales

Snow provides a critical water resource for over a billion people world wide, exerts strong controls on the Earth's energy balance, drives the winter sports industry, and has potential to bring daily commerce to a standstill during severe winter storms. Many of these wide-reaching implications are also important at smaller scales and encompass the conditions experienced by individuals and wildlife. For these reasons, our team at the University of New Hampshire Earth Systems Research Center is exploring new ways to quantify the drivers of variability in snowpacks over small distances (<1km). This winter (2023-2024), this effort takes us to Kingman Farm, a 'natural laboratory' we are using to monitor snow variability across small distances.
